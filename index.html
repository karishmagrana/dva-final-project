New York Times Trending News: Dashboard Marketing Analysis

Team004, CSE 6242: Data and Visual Analytics

The following dashboards were created using Tableau.
These dashboards include different visualizations that are aimed to identify geolocations and tags of articles from a news reporting company to help a user or company identify certain biases in their reporting techniques and choices.
The dashboards are also directly available at Tableau public.

Article Counts by Section Type

This dashboard shows the distribution of articles based on section, subsection and description. By looking at these distributions, news agencies can understand what the most trending topics currently are and determine if their articles are too focused on certain areas or if there are other areas they should focus more on.

Article Counts by Details

This dashboard provides an overview of article counts based on article details such as section, type (material and general) and kicker. By looking at type, they can understand the main ways users are consuming content. This could help with marketing efforts by allowing them to make decisions on what media types are working or not working with their consumer base.

Most Mentioned

The most mentioned dashboard gives an overview of the most commonly mentioned authors, people and organizations. Each graph is ordered from highest to lowest frequency so the viewer can easily gauge most mentioned in each category. This could be helpful for news agencies to see what people and organizations are currently trending in their articles and which authors are contributing the most.

Distinct Authors by Country

This dashboard shows a map of authors by country to easily visualize where the majority of authors are from. This can help news agencies identify and understand potential geographical biases better

Number of Sections Represented per Country

This dashboard shows a map of unique sections by country to easily visualize the diversity of news publications from each country. This will help news agencies understand which locations require greater diversity in their new publications, allowing them to reach new markets and reduce geographical biases.

Cities Represented in the Article Description Section

This dashboard shows a map of cities that are directly mentioned within an article’s description. This will help news agencies identify which cities or locations are commonly published and which regions tend to be underrepresented, allowing for greater geographic diversity and representation in article topics.

About the Project

Journalism is currently facing a turning point, as the sheer volume of data and information from the Internet complicates publishing decisions and effective storytelling. To simplify topic selection and reduce geographic bias in publishing, the members of Team 004 wanted to examine trending topics over time with a focus on geographic location. To accomplish this, a visualization was constructed to illustrate overall publishing from different regions, along with a trend analysis based on geographic region. Using this information, news organizations will be able to ensure they are publishing stories from underrepresented regions while also better tailoring coverage towards different regions. For our project, we decided to use the New York Times APIs: Most Popular, Times Newswire, and Top Stories by first using API keys to access the endpoint. Once the raw data was acquired, extensive cleaning was performed to remove duplicate or missing parameter lines, along with unsuitable lines such as poor geographic tags. Once cleaned, the data was transformed into a form suitable for Tableau, and it was then imported into Tableau for visualization generation. Within Tableau, various filtering, grouping, and aggregation processes were performed to create different visualization topics, and these were finally published on Tableau Online to be displayed.
